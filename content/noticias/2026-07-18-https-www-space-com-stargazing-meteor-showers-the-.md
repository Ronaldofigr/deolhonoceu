Their interaction with the air heats and illuminates the material and the surrounding region, creating a trail that may last only an instant. A meteor shower occurs when Earth passes through an area of space containing a greater concentration of such particles. The effect resembles parallel railway tracks that seem to meet at the horizon: the tracks do not actually converge, but perspective makes them look that way.
